        <section id="symptoms">

            <h2>Symptoms</h2>

            <p>Dependent Personality Disorder (DPD) involves pervasive dependence on others to meet emotional and physical needs. This can manifest in various ways:</p>

            <ul>

                <li><strong>Difficulty making decisions:</strong> May rely excessively on others for guidance, affecting their ability to make independent life choices.</li>

                <li><strong>Fear of abandonment:</strong> Anxiety about losing relationships may lead to clingy or submissive behavior.</li>

                <li><strong>Lack of confidence:</strong> Avoids responsibility and lacks self-assurance in personal and professional tasks.</li>

                <li><strong>Difficulty being alone:</strong> May feel helpless or distressed when not in a relationship.</li>

                <li><strong>Excessive need for reassurance:</strong> Constantly seeks approval and advice from others, which can strain relationships.</li>

            </ul>

            <p><em>Real-life impact:</em> These symptoms can affect areas like career (avoiding leadership roles), personal relationships (clinging to toxic dynamics), and mental health (increased risk of depression or anxiety).</p>

        </section>

 

        <section id="treatment">

            <h2>Treatment</h2>

            <p>The primary treatment approaches for DPD are:</p>

            <ul>

                <li><strong>Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT):</strong> Helps individuals identify and change negative thought patterns and build independence.</li>

                <li><strong>Psychodynamic Therapy:</strong> Focuses on uncovering underlying emotional conflicts driving dependent behaviors.</li>

                <li><strong>Group Therapy:</strong> Encourages building healthy relationships and assertiveness in a supportive environment.</li>

                <li><strong>Medication:</strong> While not a primary treatment, antidepressants or anti-anxiety medication may be prescribed to address co-occurring conditions.</li>

            </ul>

        </section>

 

        <section id="prognosis">

            <h2>Prognosis</h2>

            <p>With proper treatment, individuals with DPD can learn to build healthy relationships and develop greater independence. Long-term therapy and support are key to recovery. While challenges like relapse may occur, many individuals achieve significant improvements in their quality of life.</p>

        </section>
